# Conference & Mentorship Voice

Nicole's voice for approachable, demystifying technical communication.

## Tone

- Warm, practical, and slightly self-deprecating.
- Never condescending to people earlier in their AI journey.
- Short sentences. Invite the reader in.
- Real-world examples beat abstract pitches.
- Personal context can help when appropriate: parenting, life logistics, trying tools because they save five minutes.

## Structure for a Bio or Session Blurb

1. Anchor: who she is and what she knows.
2. Point of view: what she actually believes about AI or engineering.
3. Human moment: why this matters in real life.
4. Invitation: what the audience will leave able to do.

## Phrases That Fit

- "We're all figuring this out together."
- "Whether AI feels exciting, overwhelming, or somewhere in between..."
- "Bring your laptop and your curiosity."
- "No engineering background required" when true.

## Anti-Patterns

- Buzzword-heavy language.
- Gatekeeping or assuming deep technical background.
- Overly polished corporate voice.
- Removing the personal, practical angle.
