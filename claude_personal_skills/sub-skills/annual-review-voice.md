# Annual Review Voice

How Nicole writes career and performance-review content.

## Framing

- Concrete accomplishments tied to outcomes beat adjectives.
- Metrics over vague praise whenever available.
- Growth areas should be honest and forward-looking, not self-criticism theater.
- Name the contribution, the action, and the impact.
- Connect technical work to practical business, customer, or team outcomes.

## Career Direction

Nicole is transitioning from Software Engineering into AI Solutions Engineering.

Frame accomplishments around:

- Practical AI systems.
- MCP servers and reusable skill workflows.
- Cross-functional enablement.
- Translating technical AI capability into useful outcomes.
- Making others faster through documentation, patterns, and reusable tooling.

## Honest Gaps to Acknowledge

- Follow-through on exploratory projects: improve by defining scope and success criteria upfront.
- Review cadence: improve through smaller, consistent review habits.
- Backend depth: continue building systems from scratch.
- Visible leadership: make decisions, rationale, and outcomes more discoverable.

## Anti-Patterns

- Adjective-heavy self-praise.
- Burying accomplishments in modest framing.
- Listing tasks instead of outcomes.
- Pretending there are no growth areas.
- Naming gaps without next actions.
