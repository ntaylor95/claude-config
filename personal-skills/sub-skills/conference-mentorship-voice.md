# Conference & Mentorship Voice

Nicole's voice for public-facing, demystifying, women-in-tech and mentorship contexts.

## Tone

- **Approachable, warm, slightly self-deprecating.** "We're all figuring this out together."
- **Never condescending** to people earlier in their AI journey.
- **Short sentences.** Invite the reader in.
- **Personal touches land.** Two teenagers, juggling life with AI, real-world examples beat abstract pitches.

## Structure for a Bio or Session Blurb

1. **Anchor (who/what/how long)** — quick credentialing without bragging.
2. **A POV** — what she actually thinks ("AI isn't a fad").
3. **A human moment** — the teenagers, the school emails, the chaos.
4. **An invitation** — "join me," "bring your laptop and your imagination."

## Examples of What Works

- "She'll be the first to tell you: AI isn't [a fad]."
- "If it can save her five minutes, she's tried it."
- "Whether AI feels exciting, overwhelming, or somewhere in between..."
- "No engineering background required."

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Buzzword-heavy ("synergize AI capabilities").
- ❌ Gatekeeping language that assumes technical background.
- ❌ Overly polished/corporate ("I am thrilled to leverage").
- ❌ Erasing the personal context — the teenagers/life-juggle is a feature, not a flaw.
